Enterprise Writing System

Building a scalable writing system across products at Equinix

Role
UX Writer ¬∑ Content Strategist
Duration
3‚Äì4 months
Scope
Cross-product writing system and style guide contribution

Context

This work took place within a large organization where multiple teams produced content at scale across platforms. Copy was authored independently, with competing priorities and fragmented communication norms, leading to inconsistent tone and terminology, engineering-first language, and slow review cycles. Without a shared strategic language or consistent patterns, maintaining clarity, cohesion, and scalability across products was a challenge.

Constraints

  • Global, technical and non-technical audience with varied product familiarity
  • Existing brand voice needed to be preserved
  • No centralized content authority
  • Solution had to be lightweight and easy to adopt

Approach

I approached this problem as a systems design challenge rather than a writing task. The goal was not to impose rules, but to reduce decision friction by clarifying principles, mapping language decisions, and introducing shared frameworks that teams could adapt without central bottlenecks.

Assess

  • Audited UI copy, documentation, and marketing content
  • Reviewed existing style documentation to identify gaps and adoption barriers
  • Mapped high-friction areas, including CTAs, error states, onboarding, and empty states

Systemize

  • Built a reusable microcopy framework covering:
    • CTAs
    • Success and error states
    • Form labels
    • Empty states
    • Onboarding flows
  • Defined voice principles emphasizing clarity, precision, and reduced jargon

Enable Adoption

  • Partnered with design and product leads to validate tone decisions
  • Created before/after examples to demonstrate impact
  • Added inclusive writing and accessibility guidance
  • Delivered templates and quick-reference patterns for daily use

Key Decisions & Rationale

  • Favored reusable patterns over rigid rules
  • Used examples to encourage adoption rather than enforcement
  • Anchored tone guidance in existing brand principles
  • Aligned naming and message hierarchy across teams

Rationale: Reduce writer workload, increase clarity, and enable users to find answers without needing support escalation.

Outcomes

  • Writing system adopted by multiple product teams
  • Improved consistency across UI and documentation
  • Designers and PMs referenced the guide during reviews
  • Reduced revision cycles due to shared language patterns

What This Demonstrates

This case reflects my approach to enterprise content strategy: designing language systems that reduce friction, scale across teams, and hold up under real operational pressure.

Takeaway

Writing systems scale best when they reduce decision friction for teams while preserving brand integrity.
