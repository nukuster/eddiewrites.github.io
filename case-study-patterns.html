Predictable Communication System

Experimental structured language system for regulated classrooms

Role
Communication Designer ¬∑ UX Writer
Duration
Iterative pilot
Scope
Instructional language system for students and staff

Context

In high-stress classroom settings, inconsistent or ambiguous language can increase cognitive load and emotional escalation. I explored how a predictable communication system might reduce this friction by standardizing instruction and redirection language.

Constraints

  • Varying regulation needs, including neurodivergent profiles
  • Language needed to be simple, predictable, and emotionally neutral
  • Guidance that was easy to remember and apply in real time

Approach

I approached this work as a language systems design problem, focusing on predictability, emotional regulation, and repeatable patterns rather than individual phrases.

Diagnose

  • Observed classroom interactions and instruction handoffs
  • Identified points of breakdown during transitions and corrective moments
  • Analyzed how language ambiguity increased emotional load

Design the System

  • Created a repeatable microcopy pattern set for instructions, expectations, and feedback
  • Developed a step-based flow for high-friction moments: Pause ‚Üí Clarify ‚Üí Check Understanding

Enable Consistency

  • Rewrote common classroom scripts using regulated, inclusive language
  • Designed patterns that prioritized predictability and emotional safety
  • Ensured language worked under time pressure and stress

Design Intent

  • Reduce student escalation during transitions
  • Increased clarity and consistency in classroom instructions
  • Improved staff confidence when redirecting behavior
  • More predictable classroom flow for students sensitive to ambiguity

What This Demonstrates

This case demonstrates how pattern-based language design can support emotional regulation, accessibility, and consistency in high-friction environments, showing the same systems thinking and strategic clarity applied in enterprise contexts.
